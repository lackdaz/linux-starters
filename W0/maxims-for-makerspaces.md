# Maxims for space/volunteer collaborators

> You can't make omelettes without breaking eggs

Famous french saying of *"on ne fait pas d'omelette sans casser des œufs"*. Adopted 'hacker' ethos of 'bias towards action', 'no positive change without sacrifice' --- an idealogue against over-cautious, institutional thinking.

MIT Version: "hack first, ask for forgiveness later"

Facebook version: "Move fast and break things" (Zuck) -> "Move fast with stable infrastructure" (Zuck, 2014)

Failures are celebrated and planned for - a strong repair culture to mitigate downtime and spares are stocked for 'incidental' repairs

---

> Kill your darling

No relics in a makerspace please. Deification of objects should embody an ethos or a collective vision. Every object needs to earn its place in a space-limited makerspace.

---

> Respect everyone's time

Zero budget isn't fun so when there's a budget, pay it backwards and downwards however small the amount is. When you can't pay for time, pay for transport or simple food/drink.

> Leave it better than you found it

Tools/tooling that are missing/broken in the dead non-working hours can really derail a prototyping sprint. Do the kind thing of informing everyone (or even better --- restocking) when you took that last nut/screw

> Here, and now

Plan for the next 6 months. The execution must be simple enough that it does not fall apart when the smart people eventually leave and move on. Must work realistically in a zero budget situation without a professionally-funded organization to manage it. Be realistic - you are not getting any younger, work is never going to 'free up', we are not going to wait for the perfect, we are going with what we have and can make work.

> Be thankful for all creative collaborations, whether successful or not

Lookng back, after all the hard work these are going to be the only things you remember fondly. Include as many people as you can to scatter the tribal knowledge.
